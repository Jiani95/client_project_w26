Intro to Make Connections

Building connections with alumni is one of the most effective ways for students to explore career paths and secure jobs or internships. Networking consistently ranks as the top way opportunities are found, and alumni are often the most valuable contacts because they share a common educational background and are more inclined to help fellow students. Alumni can offer first-hand insight into specific industries and companies, provide honest perspectives on roles and career trajectories, and share practical advice on how to position yourself as a UMSI student to become more competitive and marketable. In fact, the number one career recommendation from the Career Development Office is to network with alumni, and that process often begins with a thoughtful email outreach.

There are few steps for you to build your Connections

  • First: Find Alumni UMSI CDO office has offered various ways and links for you to find Alumni

    Those links would be helpful for you

    • UMSI Linkedin group

      This is a Linkedin group which most UMSI alumni have joined. By searching inside the group, you can easily find alumni and communicate and chat with them.

    • UMSI Career link

      Login with your UMSI CareerLink SSO to get the access of UMSI career development resources website that's facing to UMSI student and alumni

    • UCAN

      University career alumni network platform, this website allows you catalyzing connections between students and professionals

  • Second: Get a Response Send messages to your alumni, and don't get discouraged by low response rate - average response rate to “cold messages” is around 20%. Each contact you connect with is an important and valuable addition to your network!

    We encourage you to schedule a coffee chat, phone call, or email conversation with alumni to build strong rapport through thoughtful outreach.

    Here are some DOs and DON'Ts to review before sending your first message

    • DOs: Keep your message concise, time-bound, and personal: respect their time by proposing a clear time frame (e.g., a 20-minute coffee chat within two weeks) and showing genuine appreciation for them as an individual.

    • DON'Ts: Do not ask for information that is readily available online, avoid sending your résumé without permission, and never begin by requesting a referral or a job: focus on building a genuine relationship first.
